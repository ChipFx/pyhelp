Zoom to Fit
===========

The **Zoom to Fit** command scales the canvas so that the entire document is
visible within the current viewport — no scrolling required.

Using Zoom to Fit
-----------------

There are three ways to invoke Zoom to Fit:

* Press :kbd:`Ctrl+0` (zero)
* Choose **View → Zoom → Zoom to Fit** from the menu bar
* Click the **Fit** button (|fit-icon|) in the zoom toolbar

.. note::

   Zoom to Fit respects your current view mode.  In Dual-Panel mode, it fits
   each panel to its own content independently.

Zoom Controls
-------------

These related shortcuts control zoom level directly:

* :kbd:`Ctrl++` — Zoom in
* :kbd:`Ctrl+-` — Zoom out
* :kbd:`Ctrl+0` — Zoom to fit
* :kbd:`Ctrl+1` — 100% (actual pixels)

You can also hold :kbd:`Ctrl` and scroll the mouse wheel to zoom continuously.

Zoom Presets
------------

.. warning::

   Zoom levels above 800% may reduce rendering performance on very large
   documents.  Consider switching to **Compact** mode to maximise canvas space
   before zooming in extensively.

Common preset zoom levels available from the **View → Zoom** sub-menu:

* 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%, 150%, 200%, 400%
